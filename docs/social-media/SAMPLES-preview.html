Building LearnX · #EdTech
The most-used feature in LearnX wasn't in our product roadmap.
It wasn't the AI tutor. Not the brain profiler. Not the revision scheduler.
It was a button.
A small button at the bottom of a practice test result screen.
"Share with Parents."
A developer added it in a Friday sprint because his daughter kept showing him her school drawings and he thought, "kids want someone to see."
We almost removed it. "Not a core feature." "Adds UI clutter."
Then the data came in.
9 lakh taps in one year.
Kids sharing 60% scores. 45% scores. 90% scores. Not just wins. Everything.
They weren't sharing results. They were saying: "See me. I'm trying."
LearnX
Why We Made It Free · #EducationForAll
People keep asking why LearnX is free.
They expect a clever business answer. A freemium model. A data play.
Here's the actual reason:
When we were testing in a school in rural Rajasthan, a girl asked if she could use the app at home.
We said yes. She asked if it cost money. We said we hadn't decided yet.
She nodded. Put the phone down. Didn't ask again.
Her teacher told us: "She assumed it would cost money. Everything good always does. She's learned not to want things she can't have."
She was 11 years old.
We went back to the office that night and took the pricing model off the whiteboard. It's been free since.

KYA AAPKO PATA THA?

Raat ko padhai karna galat nahi hai.

Research says some children's brains are naturally more active after 9 PM. It's called a "late chronotype." If your child studies better at night — trust their body clock.

SMART PARENT TIP

Marks mat poochho. Yeh poochho.

Instead of "kitne marks aaye?" try: "Aaj kya naya seekha?" or "Kaunsa question interesting laga?" It shifts your child's brain from fear of judgment to love of learning.

EXAM SEASON

Last 30 days mein yeh mat karo.

1) Naya topic mat shuru karo. 2) Raat bhar jaag ke mat padho — neend memory fix karti hai. 3) Dusron se compare mat karo. Apna revision plan follow karo. Bas.

FUNNY BUT TRUE

Sharma ji ka beta vs Reality

Sharma ji ka beta: "I only studied 2 hours." Translation: 2 hours of focused revision + 6 hours he's not counting + coaching notes + extra tuition. Beta, define "only."

SMART PARENT TIP

Galti pe daanto mat. Galti pe clap karo.

When your child makes a mistake and FINDS it themselves — that's a bigger win. Say: "Achha pakda! Chalo ab fix karte hain." Watch their confidence double.
