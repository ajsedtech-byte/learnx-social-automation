### LinkedIn #1: "The Fraction Error That Changed Everything" — Building LearnX

We were three months into building LearnX.

Our AI engine could grade answers instantly.

Fast. Accurate. Impressive demo.

Then we looked closer at the data.

61 lakh students answered 1/2 + 1/3 = 2/5.

Not a typo. Sixty-one lakh. Same wrong answer.

They weren't guessing. They were applying a rule.

A rule that made perfect sense to them:
add the tops, add the bottoms.

It's logical. It's consistent. It's completely wrong.

And no one caught it. Not in Class 5. Not in Class 6. Not in Class 7.

By Class 8, they'd decided they were "bad at Maths."

They weren't bad at Maths.
They had one broken concept from three years ago.

That night, we scrapped our grading engine.

We started building something different:
a system that doesn't just catch wrong answers.
It catches the wrong understanding behind them.

We called it the Mistake Genome.

It took 14 months longer than the grading engine would have.

But when a kid now gets a fraction wrong,
LearnX doesn't just say "incorrect."
It says: "I think you're adding numerators and denominators separately. Let's fix that."

That's the difference between an app that grades
and an app that teaches.

It started with a fraction.
It changed everything about how we build.

### LinkedIn #6: "The Revision Lie" — Thought Leadership

Here's something most students and parents don't know:

Only 11% of students revisit a topic
after learning it the first time.

Not 11% on LearnX. 11% everywhere.
That's a well-documented cognitive science finding.

Your child learns photosynthesis on Tuesday.
By Friday, 70% of it is gone.
By next month, it might as well have never happened.

This is the Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve.
Published in 1885.
141 years ago.

And yet our entire education system
is still built on the "teach once, test later" model.

We teach a chapter.
We move to the next chapter.
We never come back.

Then we're shocked when students "forget everything"
right before the exam.

They didn't forget because they're lazy.
They forgot because that's what brains do.

The solution isn't "study harder."
The solution is spaced repetition:
revisiting the same concept at increasing intervals
until it moves from short-term to long-term memory.

R1 to R10 — ten revision cycles
