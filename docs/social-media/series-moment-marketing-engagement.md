# Series: Moment Marketing Templates & Engagement Posts

> Two high-impact series designed for organic virality. Part 1 reacts to predictable cultural moments with Amul/Fevicol-style topicals. Part 2 generates massive comment threads through participation-driven formats.

---

## PART 1: Moment Marketing Templates

**Strategy:** React to 20 predictable Indian moments with sharp, bilingual, education-angled topicals. Post within 4 hours of the moment breaking. The goal is to become "that education brand" that always has the cleverest take.

**Design principles:**
- Bold text, minimal design, one visual metaphor max
- Bilingual puns (Hindi-English) wherever natural
- Education angle must feel like a punchline, not a pitch
- No product screenshots, no CTAs in the main visual
- Brand logo: small, bottom-right corner

### Moment #20: Any Viral Meme Template (Generic Framework)

**Timing:** Within 2-4 hours of a meme format going viral (monitor Twitter trending, Instagram Explore, Reddit)
**Visual concept:** Use the EXACT meme format that's trending — same template, same layout — but replace the content with an education/exam/study angle. The familiarity of the format does the heavy lifting.
**Main copy:** [Adapt the meme's structure with study context]
**Punchline:** [The education twist that makes people screenshot and share]
**LearnX connection:** Subtle — the brand is present in the format, not shoved in. Logo in corner, brand tone in the voice.
**Platform:** Instagram (static), Twitter (this is where memes travel fastest), Facebook

**FRAMEWORK FOR ANY VIRAL MEME:**

**Step 1: Identify the meme within 1 hour of it trending**
- Monitor: Twitter trending, Instagram Explore, r/IndianMemes, WhatsApp forwards
- Filter: Is this meme relatable to students/parents/education? If yes, proceed.

**Step 2: Map the meme's structure**
- What is the joke format? (comparison, unexpected twist, escalation, relatable pain)
- What makes it funny? (surprise, truth, exaggeration)
- What is the template? (image macro, text tweet, carousel, video)

**Step 3: Replace the content, keep the structure**
- Original: "Nobody: / Me at 3 AM: [something random]"
- LearnX version: "Nobody: / Me at 3 AM the night before boards: 'Yeh chapter toh padha hi nahi'"
- The structure stays IDENTICAL. Only the content changes.

**Step 4: Add the LearnX twist**
- The punchline should make the student think: "This is literally me AND this brand gets me."
- Don't add a CTA. Don't add "Download LearnX." The relatability IS the marketing.

**Step 5: Post and engage**
- Post within 4 hours of the meme trending (after 6 hours, the moment is dead)
- Reply to every comment in the first 30 minutes
- Share to Stories with "Tag someone who does this"

**Example — "Describe yourself in one Bollywood dialogue" trend:**
- LearnX version: "'Mogambo khush hua' — me when I see the exam paper and I've actually studied everything."
- Caption: "Tell us your exam-day Bollywood dialogue. We'll go first."

**Example — Drake "Yes/No" meme format:**
- No: "Studying the whole syllabus in one night"
- Yes: "Studying 30 minutes daily on LearnX and actually remembering it"
- Caption: "Drake knows. You should too."

## Moment Marketing: Operations Playbook

**The 4-Hour Rule:**
Every moment marketing post MUST go from "event happened" to "post live" in under 4 hours. Here's the workflow:

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| T+0 | Event happens. Social media manager flags it in the team Slack channel. |
| T+30 min | Copywriter drafts 2-3 options. Designer starts on visual concept. |
| T+1 hr | Review: Is it funny? Is the education angle natural? Is it offensive in ANY way? |
| T+2 hr | Final copy + visual ready. Legal/brand check (60-second gut check, not a committee). |
| T+3 hr | Post goes live across all platforms. |
| T+3.5 hr | Team actively replies to every comment for 30 minutes. |
| T+4 hr | Boost post if organic engagement exceeds 2x average within first hour. |

**Kill switches — do NOT post if:**
- The event involves death, tragedy, or national mourning
- The topic is politically divisive (elections, religion, caste)
- The meme could be misread as mocking students or parents
- The moment has already been hijacked by 10+ brands (you're too late)

## PART 2: "Wrong Answers Only" + Engagement Posts

**Strategy:** Generate massive comment threads by asking questions every Indian student or parent has an opinion on. The goal is NOT to sell — it's to make the comments section so entertaining that the algorithm pushes the post to 10x more people.

**Design principles:**
- Bold white/yellow text on dark background (black, dark blue, or dark green)
- ONE question per post — no clutter
- The question itself should make someone laugh or say "oh god, this is so true"
- LearnX branding: subtle watermark or logo, never the focus
- Starter comment from LearnX account is CRITICAL — it sets the tone and gives people permission to be funny

- Wrong Answers Only: Every Wednesday at 7 PM (mid-week, students are online, need a break)
- Finish This Sentence: Every Saturday at 11 AM (weekend morning, relaxed scrolling mode)
- This or That: Every Sunday at 5 PM (Sunday evening, peak engagement before Monday dread)

**Engagement protocol — first 60 minutes after posting:**

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| T+0 | Post goes live. LearnX drops the starter comment immediately. |
| T+5 min | Reply to the first 5 comments with funny, on-brand responses. |
| T+15 min | Pin the funniest comment and reply: "We have a winner." |
| T+30 min | Share 2-3 best comments to Stories with "These comments are unhinged." |
| T+45 min | Drop a second comment: "The comments did NOT disappoint. Keep going." |
| T+60 min | If engagement is above average, boost the post with a small budget targeting students 14-22. |

**Reply tone guide:**
- Be the funny friend, not the brand. No corporate language.
- Use "bhai," "yaar," "matlab," "sach mein" naturally.
- React with: "DEAD," "This is too real," "Screenshot liya, evidence hai," "Bhai, FBI open up."
- NEVER correct someone's Hindi/English. NEVER be preachy. NEVER push the product in comments.
- If someone mentions a competitor or asks about LearnX, reply with humour first, info second: "Padhai ki baat kar rahe ho? DM karo, bohot kuch batana hai."

**Content recycling:**
- Best comments become their own posts (with permission/credit): "You said it, not us" series
- Top-performing engagement posts get remade every 3 months with slight variations
- Comments that become mini-viral get screenshot-shared to Stories weekly
