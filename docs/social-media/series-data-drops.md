## Visual Template (all 15 posts)

- **Background:** Solid black (#0a0a0a)
- **Big Number:** White, massive font (70%+ of the frame)
- **Context line:** Light gray (#94a3b8), small
- **Punchline:** Teal (#2dd4bf), medium-bold, below the number
- **LearnX logo:** Small, bottom-right, 0.6 opacity
- **Format:** 1080x1080 (Instagram square) + 1080x1920 (Story) + text-only version (Twitter/WhatsApp)

## Posting Strategy

**Frequency:** 1 per week (Wednesday) — builds anticipation, becomes a "thing" people look forward to.

**Rollout:**
- Week 1-4: Posts #1, #7, #8, #2 (the universally relatable ones first)
- Week 5-8: Posts #5, #13, #9, #10 (the parent-shareable ones)
- Week 9-12: Posts #4, #11, #6, #14 (the exam-season relevant ones)
- Week 13-15: Posts #12, #3, #15 (the big finishers)

**Platform-specific tweaks:**
- **Instagram:** Full visual card + carousel with a second slide showing the LearnX feature that connects to the stat
- **Twitter/X:** Text-only version of the stat + punchline (no image — raw tweets perform better)
- **WhatsApp:** Image card optimized for forwarding (no caption needed, everything is on the image)
- **Facebook:** Full visual + longer caption for parent audience (add "Share if your child does this too")

**Engagement hooks (post in comments):**
- "What time do YOU actually start studying? Be honest."
- "Tag someone who does this."
- "Parents — what's your child's peak study hour?"
- "Drop your most replayed topic below."

---

## Tone Reminder

These posts are funny, not mean. Self-aware, not self-promotional. The humor comes from universal truths about Indian student life — not from mocking anyone. Every punchline should make someone say "haha, that's literally me" and then hit share.

The data is the hook. The punchline is the share trigger. The brand is just the quiet logo in the corner.

> "Blinkit didn't go viral because they sold groceries. They went viral because they told India things India already knew — but hadn't said out loud yet."
> That's our playbook.
